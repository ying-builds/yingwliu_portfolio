What you're comparing:

A · Current — Instrument Serif + DM Sans with your burnt red accent. Warm, editorial, personal.

B · Fraunces — Fraunces has a "wonky" quality that gives it personality without being stuffy. PT Mono on labels adds a technical/structured feel. Inter for body copy is clean and disappears. This combo says "creative but precise."

C · TM Blue — Newsreader + Inter with Ticketmaster's brand blue as the accent. This is what it looks like if you match the company's brand. Cleaner, more corporate, but the case study loses your identity.

D · Playfair — Playfair Display is higher contrast and more dramatic than Instrument Serif. PT Mono on the labels, DM Sans for body. Still your brand colors. This feels more luxe/portfolio-forward.

On the branding question: I'd recommend keeping your own brand system (A, B, or D) across all portfolio pages. The Ticketmaster blue already shows up naturally in your screenshots and photos — you don't need to bring it into the structural design. Your portfolio should feel like you, with the company's brand showing through the work itself.
